A lot can be said about 2025. It was hard, fun, interesting, and chaotic. Thankfully, though, this isn't a year in review; rather, it's a year of _music_ in review. I wanted to encapsulate my musical preferences this year in the form of a carefully curated playlist. I hope you enjoy!

## Playlist Premise

> [!WARNING]
> Spoiler alert! If you want to listen to the playlist first without spoiling the intrigue *and tracks themselves*, do so now.

The playlist premise is rather simple and is as follows:

- Start off slow, go all hopeful and positive, mostly niche indie music.
- Add multiple "sudden emotional pits" to keep things interesting, more so being a metaphorical thing.
- Reach catharsis with some well known songs.
- Rapidly reduce energy afterwards.
- End with something beautiful.

![Graph chart with three criteria: BPM, Energy, and Loud (Db). BPM goes up and down with regular intervals relatively smoothly, Energy goes from low to high, with three drops in the first half, and Loud (Db) is smooth except in the places of said Energy drops.](graph.png)

Additionally, I have set a limit for myself: only two (2) songs are permitted per artist, since otherwise playlist would be way too long. Might sound like an easy enough limitation, but it's really not, it was hard to choose only two songs from artists that I like :c

## Trivia

- Cover for the playlist is fully custom; shot on my old Kodak camera, ruined to look cool, and noised to obilivion. I had to reverse engineer it later in Figma since the initial version was in a very low resolution (300x300px) which wasn't enough for a 1920x960px banner.
- Initially I spent a long time sorting the playlist by hand to play without stitches with gapless playback and 1s crossfade, but of course it wasn't feasible, I only managed to do this with 1/3rd of it, the rest? I had no idea how to organize. I however had a very clear goal in mind; start with Good Morning, use Maebi tracks as this cool metaphorical thing, put microchip somewhere unexpectedly, and end with Look at the Sky and Ballade pour Adeline. With this goal I tasked Claude to sort the playlist based on energy values in CSV that I acquired from [chosic's Spotify playlist sorter](https://www.chosic.com/spotify-playlist-sorter) (it has a lot more values, but energy made the most sense to me), and respect my initial goals that I outlined earlier, and after a couple attempts I had a smooth energy growth with three drops, just like I wanted. Point is, Claude had no say in the creative regard, didn't pick songs for me, or remove any, and was used solely as a tool for processing existing data. As for the blog post itself it is 100% outlined and written by me. No AI proofreading, no suggestions, no nothing.
- This is not the first time my "music taste" is being mentioned on this blog; I mentioned Vylet Pony in [New Era](@/blog/2025-07-16-new-era/index.md), and PrinceWhateverer in [Rewriting My Neocities Website Was Fun!](@/blog/2024-06-04-rewriting-neocities-website/index.md).
